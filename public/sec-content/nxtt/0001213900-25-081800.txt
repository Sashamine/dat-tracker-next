<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-align: justify"><b>Item 3.01. Notice of Delisting of Failure to
Satisfy a Continued Listing Rule or Standard; Transfer of Listing.</b></p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt; text-align: justify">&#160;</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; text-align: justify">On August 25, 2025, the Company received a letter
(the &#8220;Notice&#8221;) from the Listing Qualifications Department (the &#8220;Staff&#8221;) of The Nasdaq Stock Market LLC (&#8220;Nasdaq&#8221;).
In the Notice, the Staff explained that it had reviewed the Company&#8217;s filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission (&#8220;SEC&#8221;),
including the Company&#8217;s annual report on Form 10-K filed on March 27, 2025, which reported that the Company had six (6) employees,
had terminated all of its operations in the People&#8217;s Republic of China (&#8220;PRC&#8221;) in June, 2024, and had dissolved its
PRC subsidiary &#8220;WeTrade Technology (Shanghai) Co., Ltd.&#8221; in July, 2024. The Staff also considered the Company&#8217;s quarterly
reports on Form 10-Q filed on May 9, 2025 and August 8, 2025, respectively, which indicated that the Company has not generated any revenue
in 2025. Based on the Staff&#8217;s review of the Company and pursuant to Nasdaq Listing Rule 5101, Nasdaq believes that the Company no
longer has an operating business and is therefore a &#8220;public shell,&#8221; and that the continued listing of its common stock is
no longer warranted. The Notice further advised that, unless the Company timely requests a hearing before a Hearings Panel (the &#8220;Panel&#8221;),
it would be subject to delisting.</p>

<p style="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; text-align: justify">&#160;</p>

<p style="text-align: justify; margin: 0; font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif">The Company disagrees with Nasdaq&#8217;s view regarding the Company&#8217;s
status as a &#8220;public shell&#8221; and accordingly, intends to timely request a hearing. The hearing request will automatically stay
any suspension or delisting action pending the hearing and the expiration of any additional extension period that may be granted by the
Panel pursuant to the hearing. There can be no assurance that the Company&#8217;s appeal of the delisting determination will be successful,
or that, if successful, the Company will be able to maintain compliance with all applicable listing criteria.</p>
